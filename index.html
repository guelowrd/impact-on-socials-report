Conclusions

What the campaign achieved:
• Reversed a 64-day follower bleed (−1,185) into 11 days of net growth (+866) on X
• 2× avg impressions per tweet vs the prior 30-day baseline
• 7.3× link click volume — the strongest intent signal in the dataset
• 2.1× LinkedIn follower growth rate, with a concentrated spike on Apr 23–24 (+25 in 2 days)
• +47% daily LinkedIn impressions during the window
• Sustained lift: post-Guardian May is still running above the pre-launch trough on both platforms
What it didn't do:
• LinkedIn page views actually dipped slightly during the campaign (26.5/day vs 31.7 baseline) — the content drove feed engagement but not page traffic
• Post-campaign May returned to baseline quickly on both platforms (~1 LI follower/day, ~4.4K avg imp/tweet). The campaign created a burst, not a permanent step-change
• X engagement rate during Guardian (3.3%) was slightly below the pre-period (3.5%) — the campaign reached more people but didn't engage them at a higher rate per impression
For the Wallet campaign: the Guardian data shows that narrative campaigns on X can reverse follower trends and generate outsized link click volume, but the effect is time-bound. The Wallet push should aim for a similar 10–14 day concentrated window. The mainnet tease created a warm audience — launching within the next 1–2 weeks rides that attention rather than starting cold.
